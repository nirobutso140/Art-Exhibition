The School of Athens

The School of Athens painting symbolizes Philosophy, one of the disciplines of the Humanities or areas of human knowledge, which the main room it is painted in, the Stanza della Segnatura, aims to represent. The other three paintings symbolize “Poetry”, “Justice”, and “Theology”

The School of Athens painting symbolizes Philosophy, one of the disciplines of the Humanities or areas of human knowledge, which the main room it is painted in, the Stanza della Segnatura, aims to represent. The other three paintings symbolize “Poetry”, “Justice”, and “Theology”

The School of Athens (Italian: Scuola di Atene) is a fresco by the Italian Renaissance artist Raphael. The fresco was painted between 1509 and 1511 as a part of Raphael's commission to decorate the rooms now known as the Stanze di Raffaello, in the Apostolic Palace in the Vatican
